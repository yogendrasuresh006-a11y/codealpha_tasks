Superstore Sales Data Visualization¶

This project uses data visualization techniques to analyze sales, profit, regional performance, and customer purchasing patterns.

The goal is to transform raw data into meaningful visual insights that support business decision-making.

Visualization 1: Sales by Category¶

In [3]:
category_sales = df.groupby("Category")["Sales"].sum()

plt.figure(figsize=(8,5))

sns.barplot(
    x=category_sales.index,
    y=category_sales.values
)

plt.title("Sales by Category")
plt.xlabel("Category")
plt.ylabel("Total Sales")

plt.show()

Insight¶

Technology generated the highest sales among all categories, indicating strong customer demand for technology products.

Visualization 3: Regional Sales Distribution¶

In [5]:
region_sales = df.groupby("Region")["Sales"].sum()

plt.figure(figsize=(7,7))

plt.pie(
    region_sales,
    labels=region_sales.index,
    autopct="%1.1f%%"
)

plt.title("Regional Sales Distribution")

plt.show()

Insight¶

The West region contributed the highest share of total sales, followed by the East region.

The South region generated the lowest sales among all regions.

This indicates that the company's strongest market presence is in the West region.

Visualization 4: Top 10 Selling Sub-Categories¶

In [6]:
top10 = (
    df.groupby("Sub-Category")["Sales"]
      .sum()
      .sort_values(ascending=False)
      .head(10)
)

plt.figure(figsize=(12,6))

sns.barplot(
    x=top10.index,
    y=top10.values
)

plt.xticks(rotation=45)

plt.title("Top 10 Selling Sub-Categories")
plt.xlabel("Sub-Category")
plt.ylabel("Total Sales")

plt.show()

Insight¶

Phones generated the highest sales among all sub-categories, closely followed by Chairs.

Storage, Tables, and Binders also contributed significantly to total revenue.

These sub-categories represent the company's key revenue drivers and should be prioritized in inventory and marketing strategies.

Visualization 5: Discount vs Profit¶

In [7]:
plt.figure(figsize=(8,5))

sns.scatterplot(
    data=df,
    x="Discount",
    y="Profit"
)

plt.title("Discount vs Profit")
plt.xlabel("Discount")
plt.ylabel("Profit")

plt.show()

Insight¶

The scatter plot shows a negative relationship between Discount and Profit.

As discounts increase, profit tends to decrease.

Several orders with very high discounts resulted in significant losses, indicating that excessive discounting can negatively impact business profitability.

This visualization supports the findings from the correlation analysis performed during EDA.

Visualization 6: Correlation Heatmap¶

In [8]:
numeric_df = df.select_dtypes(include="number")

plt.figure(figsize=(8,5))

sns.heatmap(
    numeric_df.corr(),
    annot=True,
    cmap="coolwarm"
)

plt.title("Correlation Heatmap")

plt.show()

Insight¶

The heatmap shows the relationships among numerical variables in the dataset.

Discount has a negative correlation with Profit, while Sales has a positive relationship with Profit.

These relationships help identify factors that influence business performance.

Visualization Summary¶

The visualizations provided valuable insights into sales performance, profitability, regional trends, and discount impacts.

Key findings include:

  1. Technology generated the highest sales and profit among all categories.

  2. The West region contributed the largest share of total sales.

  3. Phones and Chairs were the top-performing sub-categories in terms of revenue.

  4. Higher discounts were associated with lower profits, highlighting the risks of excessive discounting.

  5. The correlation heatmap confirmed important relationships among sales, profit, quantity, and discount variables.

These visualizations transformed raw sales data into meaningful business insights.

Business Recommendations¶

  1. Increase focus on Technology products, as they generate the highest sales and profit.

  2. Review discount strategies to avoid excessive discounts that negatively impact profitability.

  3. Prioritize high-performing products such as Phones, Chairs, and Storage items.

  4. Improve sales performance in lower-performing regions, particularly the South region.

  5. Monitor low-profit products and optimize pricing and promotional strategies.

  6. Use data-driven decision-making to improve inventory management and marketing campaigns.

Conclusion¶

This project successfully transformed raw retail sales data into meaningful visual insights using Python, Matplotlib, and Seaborn.

The analysis revealed that Technology is the strongest category in terms of both sales and profitability. Regional analysis showed that the West region contributes the highest revenue, while product-level analysis identified Phones and Chairs as major revenue drivers.

The Discount vs Profit analysis demonstrated that excessive discounting can reduce profitability, emphasizing the importance of effective pricing strategies.

Overall, the visualizations provided actionable insights that can support business decision-making and improve organizational performance.
